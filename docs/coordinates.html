V13 Coordinate System (Current)

Angular coordinate (corrected 2026-03-28): The EW angle in the dome frame is simply the negation of geographic east longitude. No UTC conversion required.

θ = −lonE

Examples:
  London     lon_E =   0.0°  →  θ =    0.0°
  Tokyo      lon_E = 139.7°  →  θ = −139.7°
  New York   lon_E = −74.0°  →  θ = +74.0°
  Sydney     lon_E = 151.2°  →  θ = −151.2°   (old formula gave +32.5° — WRONG by 183°)
  Cape Town  lon_E =  18.4°  →  θ =  −18.4°
  Buenos Aires lon_E = −58.4°  →  θ = +58.4°

Physical basis: Solar noon UTC ≈ 12 − lonE/15h, so θ = (noon_UTC − 12) × 15° = −lonE. The old formula was correct for NH cities but gave +32.5° for Sydney (NH-equivalent position at lon=+32.5°) instead of the true θ = −151.2°. This was a 183° error that caused all cross-equatorial distances to fail.

Two-zone disc topology: NH and SH cities are on opposite sides of the equatorial ring at r = 14,105 km. SH disc radius is the equatorial reflection of the NH radius.

r_NH = solve:  r × tan(polaris_elevation) = H(r)   [inner zone, 0 to r_eq]
r_SH = 2 × r_eq − r_NH                            [outer zone, reflected through equatorial ring]

r_eq = 14,105 km   (equatorial ring radius)
H(r) = 8537 × exp(−r / 8619) km

Example (Sydney, lat 33.9°S):
  r_NH for lat 33.9°N = 7,193 km
  r_SH = 2 × 14,105 − 7,193 = 21,017 km

Physical basis: The equatorial ring is the mirror plane of the disc. NH cities cluster inward from it; SH cities are at equivalent angular distance outward. The firmament’s ovoid shape (V13) makes the outer zone physically distinct from the inner zone — the two zones are separated by the equatorial ring, not just reflected.

Tesla distance formula (V13):

NS  = r2 − r1   (if same hemisphere), or r1 + (2×r_eq − r2) via reflection
EW  = r_avg × Δθ × f_A(r_avg)   (aetheric EW compression)

f_A(r) = 1 − (1 − b/a) × (1 − exp(−r/λg)) / (1 − exp(−a/λg))

d    = sqrt(NS² + EW²) / n(r_avg)   (divide by aetheric refraction index)

Parameters (locked 2026-03-28):
  r_eq = 14,105 km   b = 19,172 km   b/a = 0.958   λg = 8,619 km
  n(r) = 1 + 0.271 × (H0/H(r) − 1)

Performance:
  NH same-hemisphere:       7.3% mean error
  SH same-hemisphere:      10.2% mean error
  Cross-equatorial (6 routes): 6.2% RMSE

⚠ METHODOLOGY NOTE: WGS84 globe distances are NOT the benchmark. WGS84 is a Category 2 measurement (derived from globe assumptions). The dome geometric distance is the baseline. Deviations between dome distance and Category 1 measurements (flight time × airspeed) are aetheric slipstream signals, not model errors.
